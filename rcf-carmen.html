INTERVIEW OF CARMEN GUZMÁN 

**TRANSLATED FROM SPANISH** 

BY OFF. CASTAÑEDA: 

Q. Good morning, Mrs..

A. What happened? Is my son okay?

Q. I’m sorry, we haven’t found her yet. But we’re searching.

A. Please, bring her home to me.

Q. We will. But right now, we need your help solving another case. Did Castel know a girl named River Ahmed?

A. Not to my knowledge.

Q. This is her. Have you seen her?

A. ...No. No, I’ve never seen her before. Why?

Q. I’ve been told that Castel knew her. Are you sure that she never mentioned a girl like her? Or perhaps a fight with her and Jeremiah?

A. A fight? That can’t be. Castel isn’t violent.

Q. Many have told me that River was involved in a fight between Castel and Jeremiah.

A. I don’t know anything about that. I’m sorry.

Q. Don’t worry about it. Do you know if Castel knew Milo Solís?

A. Huh? Paloma and David’s son?

Q. That’s correct.

A. No, Castel doesn’t know him.

Q. So they didn’t have any kind of relationship?

A. No, of course not. Solís is of the high class, while we’re barely getting by.

Q. Do you think that could be why Castel ran away?

A. I don’t know. She worried a lot about our financial situation, but if that were the reason, I think she’d have left sooner.

Q. Were there other problems that could’ve pushed her to run away?

A. Um... She and I didn’t always get along. I love my son with all of my heart, but sometimes... she reminds me of my ex-husband. They don’t look alike at all, but sometimes she says something, and her voice sounds like her father’s to me. The truth is, I didn’t want kids because I knew I couldn’t be a good mother. But I got pregnant by accident, and José wouldn’t let me get an abortion. So, now I have a son, and I swear I love her, but it hurts. And because of me, she’s hurting, too. I think she hates me for all the suffering I’ve caused her. I think that’s why she ran away. But oh, how I love her, officer. I can’t live without her. Please, bring her back to me. I won’t make her suffer anymore.

Q. We’re doing everything we can to find her, Mrs..

A. Please. I didn’t mean to hurt her.

Q. I know, Mrs.. She must know it, too.

A. No, I don’t think she does. I want her to know.

Q. Calm down. We’ll find her, and you’ll be able to tell her then.

A. ...I’m sorry.

Q. It’s okay. I understand that this is hard for you.

A. It really is.

Q. Get some rest. We’ll finish this another day. Okay?

A. Yes. Yes, let’s do that.
