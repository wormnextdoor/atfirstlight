INTERVIEW OF ALTHEA AHMED 

BY DET. LEBLANC: 

Q. Good afternoon, Mrs. Ahmed. Thank you for coming. I’m sorry for your loss.

A. Thank you, officer, for your service.

Q. I’m just doing my job. Making sure no one suffers the same fate as your daughter. Can you tell me about her?

A. River was... Gods, where do I start? She was energetic, disorganized and chaotic. When it started getting late and she hadn’t gotten home yet, I didn’t question it. She tended to lose track of time or get distracted or get lost on the walk home. It wasn’t weird for her to arrive much later than I had asked her to. What was weird was how she didn’t answer the phone. She was so kind and considerate, always going out of her way to help others, even strangers. She knew that if I was calling, it was because I was worried about her, so she made sure to always pick up or call me back as soon as she could. So, when she didn’t... that’s when I knew something was wrong.

Q. And you’re sure she didn’t mention who she was going to see or where she planned on meeting them?

A. Yes, I’m sure. If she had mentioned it, I’d recall it, or my husband would, but neither of us has a clue. All she said was that she was going to hang out with her friend. I assumed that she was going on a walk around the island with Joan.

Q. Joan?

A. Her girlfriend.

Q. River was gay?

A. She was, yes.

Q. Was she very open about it?

A. Yes. River was a very loud person in general, especially about things she cared about. She loved Joan dearly, and didn’t want to be quiet about it. She was very proud of who she was.

Q. Do you know of anyone who could've wanted to hurt her because of that?

A. No, I can’t think of anyone who might want to hurt her. She was very open about everything; she would’ve told me if she was in any kind of danger, I know it. But she never mentioned any enemies or someone trying to attack her, physically or otherwise. There was only one recent occasion that I know of where she got into a fight, but the fight really didn’t involve her. She said she saw someone being attacked, and she intervened.

Q. Do you know the names of the people involved?

A. ...She only mentioned one name, the name of the boy who was being attacked, but I can’t recall it anymore. I think it started with a J. But I don’t know the attacker’s name.

Q. What about her friends? Who did she usually hang out with?

A. She had lots of friends, but I think her closest ones were Joan and Florian.

Q. Do you know their last names, or where we can find them?

A. Yes, of course. I can give you their parents’ phone numbers. It’s Joan Ripley and Florian Elster. But... Florian passed away recently.

Q. I’ve heard. They committed suicide, correct?

A. They did, yes. It was really hard on River. She hated doing chores, but after their passing, she started taking on all the housework. I think she was trying to keep herself busy so she wouldn’t have time to think about it. But I managed to talk her into going out with her friends again, and I think she started getting better.

Q. Alright. Thank you, Mrs. Ahmed.

A. Thank you, officer. Good luck.
