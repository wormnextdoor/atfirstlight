INTERVIEW OF ROWAN MITCHELL 

BY DET. LEBLANC: 

Q. Pleasure to meet you, Rowan.

A. The pleasure’s all mine, detective.

Q. I hear you’re a friend of River Ahmed’s.

A. I was. I was the last person to see her alive, actually.

Q. Oh?

A. The day she disappeared, she showed up at my house. No one else was home. I let her inside, and we hung out for a while. I was planning on contacting you soon to tell you.

Q. Well, now’s your chance.

A. Nothing interesting happened. We just talked about all sorts of things. Last time I saw her, she’d been worried about her friend, so I asked her about it, and she gave me an update on that.

Q. Do you know their name?

A. Milo Solis.

Q. River was friends with Milo Solis?

A. That’s correct.

Q. I see. Go on.

A. We were having a good time, and it was getting late, so I invited her to stay for the night. She was reluctant at first—said she didn’t want to be a bother—but eventually agreed. So, when my parentz got home, I left to try to talk them into it. When I came back, she was gone. All she left was a note saying that she had something to take care of, but she’d be right back. Only she never returned. I assumed that she changed her mind and went home after all.

Q. Could I see the note?

A. Sure. I’ve got it right here.

Q. ...Mind if I keep this?

A. Not at all.

Q. Thanks.

Q. Do you know anything about an investigation River was conducting related to Florian Elster’s suicide?

A. No, I don’t. She didn’t mention it.

Q. What about a fight?

A. Nope. Didn’t mention that, either.

Q. You wouldn’t happen to know where I can find Jeremiah Lowley then, would you?

A. ‘Fraid not.

Q. Alright. Thank you for your time. That’s all for now.

A. Of course. Good luck, detective.
