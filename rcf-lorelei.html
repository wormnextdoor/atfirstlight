INTERVIEW OF LORELEI ELSTER 

BY DET. LEBLANC: 

Q. Lorelei Elster. Have a seat.

A. I know what this is about. I promise you, my family had nothing to do with River’s death.

Q. Tell me about Florian.

A. They were... a free spirit. They loved the world, even though they knew firsthand how cruel it could be.

Q. Yet they left it behind.

A. They had a lot of trouble at home, especially with their mother. A lot of it had to do with their identity—Gracie had a certain image of them that she forced them to fit, even though it wasn’t who they were at all. There were a lot of other problems too, and it all piled up, and... They lost hope. Ran off one night and overdosed. We found them dead a week later.

Q. My condolences.

A. It’s fine. This isn’t about them. About River—I spoke to her the morning she went missing.

Q. Oh? Tell me about that.

A. I called her. This Quinn person she was hanging out with claimed to have been a friend of Florian’s, only they didn’t know anyone named Quinn. I tried to warn her to stay away from the girl, but she wouldn’t listen.

Q. Do you know where I could find Quinn, or her full name?

A. I’m afraid not. But I can describe her for you.

Q. Go ahead.

A. She had wavy ginger hair reaching up to her shoulders, white skin and a round face. I think her eyes were... brown, maybe? Or hazel? I don’t recall. But she was about as tall as me, so around 165 centimeters.

Q. ...Great. Thank you. Now, tell me: were you aware that River was investigating Florian’s death as a potential murder on her own terms?

A. Yes, I was. I knew she would before she even started from the look on her face at their funeral. She thought I was lying to her about their cause of death.

Q. And you allowed her to conduct the investigation?

A. I did. I knew the truth, and I thought that soon enough, she’d realize it, too. I know her family has always struggled with grief, so I understood where she was coming from and let her be. I didn’t help her though, especially not after she tried to accuse me of murder.

Q. Was Quinn involved?

A. I think they were working together, yes. I don’t know why Quinn was pretending to have been a friend of Florian’s, though. Maybe she was just some random nosey teenager.

Q. Do you know of anyone else that got involved in the investigation? Maybe people that River contacted or suspected of having killed Florian?

A. She reached out to Florian’s parents, and...

A. ...that’s all I know.

Q. A few weeks before her disappearance, River got involved in a fight. Do you know anything about that?

A. A fight... Possibly? I think I might know about that, yes.

Q. Do you know who was involved? Perhaps someone by the name of Jeremiah?

A. Jeremiah was involved?

Q. You know him?

A. I’ve heard of him. He used to bully Florian in primary school.

Q. I see. Do you know where I can find him?

A. Sorry, I don’t. But if it helps, I believe his full name is Jeremiah Lowley.

Q. That does help. Thank you. That’s all for now. I’ll let you know if anything else comes up.

A. Thank you.
