INTERVIEW OF JOAN RIPLEY 

BY DET. LEBLANC: 

Q. Thank you for coming, Joan. Please, have a seat.

Q. Can you tell me about your relationship with River?

A. Oh, yeah, of course. We started dating I think... last year? Yeah, last December. We first met that same year at school. We didn't really get along at first because of this big misunderstanding, but eventually we cleared it up and... yeah. She was kind and thoughtful and supportive... everything I ever could’ve asked for and more. I didn’t deserve her.

Q. Why do you say that?

A. It’s just that... I really struggle with social anxiety, which shouldn’t have matched well with how outgoing and extroverted she was, but we made it work mostly because of her efforts. She never judged me or pushed me too far. We went to a few big social events together, and if she saw that I was overwhelmed or anxious or just not having a good time, she always made up an excuse to leave, even if she wanted to stay longer. I could tell—she was a horrible liar. And now, recently, when she needed support... I couldn’t be that for her.

Q. What was troubling her?

A. Our mutual friend, Florian, committed suicide recently. She had a lot of trouble accepting that. Not like, struggling to accept that they’re dead, but... She was convinced that they were murdered.

Q. Did she mention why?

A. She insisted that the story wasn’t adding up. It made perfect sense to me, so I figured she was just grieving in her own way. But then she started looking into it, and that got me really worried about her. I just... don’t think that’s a normal and healthy reaction to loss, y’know? So, I tried to get her to drop it. She said she would, but then she didn’t, and we had this fight over the phone... then, out of nowhere, she texted me an apology. I replied, but she never saw it. And then, next thing I know, her parents are calling me asking if I’ve seen her.

Q. At what time did you receive the message?

A. It was nighttime. I think... around 7 or 8?

Q. What did it say?

A. Just something along the lines of “You were right, I’m sorry.” I can show you if you’d like. The timestamp should be on there, too.

Q. Please.

A. Here.

Q. ...Thank you.

Q. Do you know anything about a fight River was involved in a few weeks before her disappearance?

A. She got in a fight? I had no idea. Oh, God, I’m so... I’m sorry.

Q. No worries. Do you know if she had any enemies?

A. No, not that I know of.

Q. What about her friends? Anyone she might’ve been hanging out with the day she disappeared?

A. She had a lot of friends. Florian, Rowan... Oh! The day she went missing, in the morning we fought on call, and she mentioned that she was going to see her friend Quinn.

Q. Quinn? Do you have a last name or a way we could contact her?

A. Sorry, I don’t. But I know they were meeting at Jonathan-Wilson primary school.

Q. Did she go there often?

A. I guess. Only recently, though. She used to never stop by, but I think she and Quinn always met up there, so she started going more often.

Q. She met Quinn recently?

A. Yeah. Not long after Florian’s death.

Q. One last thing: you mentioned someone named Rowan. Do you have a last name or contact number?

A. Oh, yeah! I don’t have his number or address or anything, but he shouldn’t be too hard to find. River’s parents will know where to find him; I think he was a family friend. But, um, his last name’s Mitchell. Rowan Mitchell.

Q. Perfect. Thank you for your time.

A. Of course.
